## Assignment Deliverables

Sew a minimum of 2 each of the following seams.

- Plain Seam
- French Seam
- Mock Flat Felled Seam
- Serged Seam
- Serged Seam Topstitched
- Busted Seam

## Assignment Overview

1. Cut out 12 4" x 7" pieces of fabric of similar weight.
2. Using two pieces of fabric sew a plain seam along the 6" side of the fabric.
   - [How to Sew a Plain Seam](../../../../sewing/how-to-sew-a-plain-seam.md)
3. Using two pieces of fabric sew a french seam along the 6" side of the fabric.
   - [How to Sew a French Seam?](../../../../sewing/how-to-sew-a-french-seam.md)
4. Using two pieces of fabric sew a mock flat felled seam along the 6" side of the fabric.
   - [How to Sew a Mock Flat Felled Seam](../../../../sewing/how-to-sew-a-mock-flat-felled-seam.md)
5. Using two pieces of fabric sew a serged seam along the 6" side of the fabric.
   - [How to Sew a Serged Seam with a Home Serger](../../../../sewing/how-to-sew-a-serged-seam-with-home-serger.md)
6. Using two pieces of fabric sew a serged seam with topstitching along the 6" side of the fabric.
   - [How to Sew a Serged Seam with Top Stitching Home Serger](../../../../sewing/how-to-sew-serged-seam-with-topstiching-home-serger.md)
7. Using two pieces of fabric sew a busted seam along the 6" side of the fabric.
   - [How to Sew a Busted Seam](../../../../sewing/how-to-sew-a-busted-seam.md)
8. Using two pieces of fabric sew a true flat felled seam along the 6" side of the fabric.
   - [How to Sew a True Flat Felled Seam](../../../../sewing/how-to-sew-true-flat-felled-seam.md)
9. Stitch all of the seams together in a line along the 6" egdes to form a strip. For joing the fabric pieces together use a variety of seams. In the end you should have a minimum of 2 examples of each type of seam.
10. Finish the edges of the strip with the serger or a folded hem. Each seam should be 7-9 inches long. The spacing between the seams should be at least an inch.
