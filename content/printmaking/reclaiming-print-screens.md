Relaiming print screens is an import part of the print process. Screen Stripper helps the power washer strip off the hardened photo emulsion from the screen. It is important to wear proper PPE as well as follwo the instructions so you don't injure yourself or damage the equipment.

1. Place your screen in the wash sink leaning against the wall. The flat side should be facing outward.
2. Prepare the power washer.
   1. Turn on the water spigot to the green hose. This hose is connected to the power washer. Do this before turning on the power washer.
   2. Put on ear and eye protection.
   3. While the power washer is still off, point the wand toward the wall, pull the trigger and hold until a steady stream of water. This removes any air from the power washer line.
   4. Ensure the power washer is plugged in.
   5. Turn on the power washer. Note: ‚èΩ=ON and ‚≠ò=OFF
   6. Test that the power washer is operational.
3. Spray Screen Stipper onto the screen. This is marked in the Red üü• spray bottles.
4. The Screen Stipper should sit on the screen for a couple minutes.
5. Once it has had time to work, then use one of the cloth rags to scrub the cleaner into the emulsion on the print screen.
6. Use the power washer to spray all of the emulsion out of the screen. If you work horizontally back and forth, starting from the bottom, this allows the Screen Stipper above to have lmore time to work.
7. Completely remove all emulsion from the screen. If needed, a bit more Screen Stripper can be sprayed.
8. Check with the Print Studio Manager that your screen is completely free of emulsion and clean.
9. Alow to dry.
10. Clean up work area.
